Pork Sisig

Pork sisig is a well-loved Filipino dish made from boiled, grilled, and chopped pork—often including pork belly or mask—mixed with onions, calamansi juice, chili peppers, and seasonings. Traditionally served on a sizzling plate, it offers a delicious blend of crispy, savory, tangy, and spicy flavors, making it a favorite companion to rice or drinks.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly (or pork mask/ear if preferred, for authentic texture)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 pcs chili peppers (siling labuyo or long green chili), chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp calamansi juice (or lemon/lime juice)
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise (optional, for creamy sisig)
  • 1 egg (optional, cracked on top while sizzling)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil or butter
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pork:
    • Boil pork belly in salted water until tender (about 30-40 minutes).
    • Drain and let it cool, then grill or pan-fry until skin is crispy.
    • Chop into small cubes.
  2. Sauté:
    • In a pan, heat oil or butter.
    • Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
    • Add chopped pork and cook for 5-7 minutes until lightly browned.
    • Add soy sauce, calamansi juice, and chopped chilies.
    • Mix well and season with salt & pepper to taste.
    • Stir in mayonnaise (optional) for a creamy version.
  4. Serve:
    • Transfer to a sizzling plate (if available).
    • Crack a fresh egg on top (optional) and mix while hot.
    • Garnish with extra onions or chilies.
