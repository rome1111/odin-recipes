Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish made with vinegared rice paired with fresh ingredients such as raw fish, seafood, or vegetables. It comes in different forms, like rolls, nigiri, or sashimi-style servings, and is often enjoyed with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger. Light, flavorful, and artfully presented, sushi is celebrated worldwide for its balance of taste and elegance.

Ingredients

For the rice:

  • 2 cups sushi rice (short-grain)
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • ¼ cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

For rolling:

  • 4-5 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 1 cucumber (cut into thin strips)
  • 1 avocado (sliced)
  • Fresh fish (salmon, tuna) or imitation crab sticks
  • Soy sauce (for dipping)
  • Wasabi and pickled ginger (optional)

Tools:

  • Bamboo sushi mat (makisu)
  • Plastic wrap (to keep rice from sticking to the mat)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice:
    • Rinse sushi rice in cold water until water runs clear.
    • Cook with water (rice cooker or stovetop).
    • Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Gently fold into warm rice. Let cool.
  2. Prepare fillings:
    • Slice cucumber, avocado, and fish into thin strips.
  3. Roll the sushi:
    • Place nori sheet (shiny side down) on bamboo mat covered with plastic wrap.
    • Spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over nori (leave 1 inch uncovered at the top).
    • Arrange fillings horizontally across the rice.
    • Roll tightly using the bamboo mat, sealing the edge with a little water.
  4. Slice and serve:
    • Cut roll into 6-8 pieces with a sharp, wet knife.
    • Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
